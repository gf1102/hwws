Mission Statement

The project set out to gather their individual, collective and collaborative stories from the community change work performed in transforming a neighborhood within the Harlem Community, 1993 to present. The work tells the stories of five Harlem Women that share their grassroots community organizing stories, their lives, their experiences, their expectations, and results from their work as community power change agents. Through this process, the women share lessons learned from their stories. This project will create an environment for grassroots people who are the subjects of community power change work, to give their own untainted and authentic accounts of their involvement in the work, through individual and collaborative reflective stories. These stories will be personal and inclusive of hopes, dreams, struggles, failures, successes; resulting in lessons from their individual and shared journey. Stories and lessons will include following categories:
  • Individual Personal and Professional Growth and Development Stories
  • Collaborative and Personal Community Power Change Stories and Lessons
  • Who they were Then and who you are Now Stories
  • What was the community like then and what is the community like now—how did / do you fit in.

Our Goals and Vision

  • Focus of project will spotlight community residents’ individual and collaborative stories of community power change work and personal growth and development while participating in the work of Harlem Children’s Zone’s Community Pride, beginning 1993 – present
  • Develop Critical events and build Story Bylines / Boards about resident involvement, community, and civic engagement, both personal and individual, 1993-present
  • Draw learning lessons from stories and work during this period to share with others

Our Core Values and Belief

  • The Importance of community members engaged in grassroots organizing that affect their lives to tell their stories in their own authentic voices
  • Great merit in using reflective and guided process methods in uncovering tacit knowledge that people hold
  • The benefits of using storytelling circles to build stories using people who have shared experiences
  • The importance of developing lessons and exercises from story experiences for models of replications
  • The importance of encouraging people in grassroots movements / organizing to record or capture their work
  • The value of deepening public awareness and understanding the way in which local citizens in neighborhoods come together not only in rebuilding grassroots methods in their community, but also forging human bonds of trust and friendship among neighbors
